## Statement

For a primal linear program and its dual, weak duality always holds, and under suitable feasibility conditions strong duality gives equal optimal objective values.

## Assumptions

Primal and dual should be formulated as a valid dual pair. Strong duality additionally needs the standard feasibility and boundedness conditions used in linear programming theory.

## Consequences

Duality provides lower or upper bounds, certificates of optimality, and sensitivity insights.

## Proof Sketch

Weak duality follows by comparing any primal-feasible solution with any dual-feasible solution. Strong duality is then obtained using the fundamental theorem of linear programming or convex separation arguments.
